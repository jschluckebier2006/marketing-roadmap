Automated Booking Confirmations

When someone books through your website or app, they should get immediate confirmation. Not tomorrow. Not when you check your email. Right now.

What They Should Receive Immediately:

Email/Text:
Booking confirmation with order number and date
Details:
What to expect, delivery window, setup instructions
Policy:
Weather policy, cancellation terms, safety guidelines
Next Steps:
What happens next, when you'll contact them, how to reach you

The Tools That Handle This:

Event Rental Systems (ERS), BookingBug, Setmore, and similar platforms automatically send confirmation messages when bookings come in. Some integrate with your website, others work standalone.

The Hard Truth:

If you're still manually confirming bookings via text or email, you're losing bookings to faster competitors. Customers expect instant confirmation. Provide it, or they'll book someone else.

Automated Follow-Up Sequences

After someone books, they shouldn't hear from you just once. A well-timed follow-up sequence keeps you top of mind and reduces no-shows.

The Perfect Follow-Up Timeline:

Day Before Event:
Reminder text/email with delivery time, what to prepare, contact number for day-of questions
Day Of Event:
"We're on our way!" notification with driver name/photo, ETA, parking instructions
Day After Event:
"How was your event?" text with review request link. Peak time to capture feedback
1 Week After:
"Thanks again! Share us with friends" + referral offer (Refer a friend, get $25 off)
1 Month After:
"Planning another event? Book early and save!" seasonal promotions for holidays

Each message has a clear purpose: reduce anxiety, increase satisfaction, generate reviews, drive referrals, encourage repeat business.

Automated Review Requests

Reviews are your most powerful marketing asset. The more reviews you have, the higher you rank in Google. The higher your rating, the more people trust you enough to book.

The Review Request System:

  • 24 hours after every event: Automatic review request text/email
  • Direct link: Make it one click to leave a review (no searching required)
  • Follow-up: One reminder if they don't respond (but don't be pushy)
  • Respond to every review: Even negative ones. Thank them, address concerns, mention keywords

Tools That Automate This:

Podium

Texting platform with built-in review request automation. Tracks which customers have left reviews.

Birdeye

Reputation management platform. Sends review requests, monitors reviews across platforms, responds automatically.

Booking Software

Most modern booking systems (Acuity Scheduling, Setmore) have review request automation built in.

Your Review Request Flow:

Event Ends → 24hrs Later → Automatic Text Sent → Customer Clicks Link → Review Posted → You Respond with Keywords → Rank Higher → More Bookings

Why this matters: 20 real reviews with 4.8 stars beats any paid ad campaign. People trust other customers more than your marketing.

Email Marketing Automation

Email is one of the highest ROI marketing channels. You already collect emails from inquiries and bookings—use them.

The Automated Email Campaigns:

  • Spring/Summer Booking Reminders: "Summer's coming! Book your bounce house early for the best dates"
  • Holiday Specials: 4th of July party season, Halloween events, holiday gatherings
  • Back-to-School Field Days: Schools need entertainment. Email every customer who has school-age kids
  • Birthday Reminders: If you collect birthdays, "Your child's birthday is coming up! Book early for the best selection"
  • Winback Campaigns: Customers who booked 1+ years ago. "We miss you! Here's 15% off to plan your next party"

Email Tools (Free to Start):

Mailchimp

Free tier: 500 contacts, unlimited emails. Pre-built templates, automation workflows, segmentation.

Constant Contact

Free trial. Email automation, customer segmentation, conversion tracking. Built for small businesses.

Brevo (Sendinblue)

Free: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts. Advanced segmentation, SMS automation, CRM integration.

The Email Automation Formula

Collect every email → Segment by interest/history → Send seasonal campaigns → Track clicks → Follow up with unclickers

A single email campaign can generate 10-15% of your monthly revenue if done right.

Remarketing & Retargeting

Someone visits your website but doesn't book. That's not a lost customer—that's a missed opportunity. Remarketing brings them back.

How It Works:

  • Website visitor → Sees your ads on Facebook/Instagram for 30 days - They already showed interest
  • Google Ads Remarketing: Follow visitors around the web with your ads on Google search, YouTube, partner sites
  • Repeat exposure = Higher conversion: Most customers need 5-7 exposures before buying. Use ads to provide those touches

What You Need to Set Up:

Step 1: Facebook Pixel

A tracking code on your website that records every visitor. Takes 10 minutes to install.

Step 2: Google Ads Remarketing Tag

Similar to Facebook Pixel. Tracks website visitors for Google Ads retargeting campaigns.

Step 3: Create Remarketing Audiences

Segment visitors: "People who viewed bounce house rentals", "People who added to cart but didn't book", etc.

Step 4: Launch Retargeting Ads

Show specific ads to each audience. "You were looking at bounce houses. Here's 10% off if you book this week."

Why This Works:

Remarketing is extremely cost-effective because you're targeting people who already showed interest. Your cost per conversion is often 70% lower than cold traffic.

CRM & Pipeline Management

A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system is where every lead, booking, and customer lives. It's your business nervous system.

What You Should Track:

  • Every lead from inquiry to booking: Know where they came from, when you followed up, what they booked
  • Repeat customers: Who are your most loyal? What do they book? When do they book?
  • Referral sources: Which customers referred you? How much revenue did they generate?
  • Follow-up tasks: Who needs a follow-up? When? What for?
  • Revenue by source: Which marketing channel (Google Ads, Facebook Marketplace, referrals) makes you the most money?

CRM Options by Stage:

Start: Google Sheets

Free. Simple tracking. Columns: Lead Name, Email, Phone, Inquiry Date, Booking Date, Amount, Referral Source. Gets you 80% of the way.

Growing: HubSpot Free

Free CRM with contact management, deal tracking, email integration. Scales with you as you grow.

Scaling: Pipedrive

$11/month. Built for sales teams. Visual pipeline, automation, reporting. Great for 5+ bookings/week.

The CRM Rule

Don't let leads fall through the cracks. If you're managing leads in your phone notes or email, you're leaving money on the table. A system ensures nothing gets missed.
